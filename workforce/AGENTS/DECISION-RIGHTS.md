# Human and AI Decision Rights

## AI may act autonomously

- Classify, retrieve, summarise, draft, compare, monitor and recommend within approved data and tool boundaries.
- Create internal artifacts and reversible workflow records.
- Route low-risk work and flag exceptions.

## AI may act only after human approval

- Send or publish external communications.
- Change authoritative records, policies, curricula, assessments or production systems.
- Commit budget, issue invoices, accept contractual terms or make public claims.
- Trigger personalised interventions based on student, staff or client data.

## Human decision is mandatory

- Academic governance, course approval, standards and awards.
- Admissions exceptions, credit/RPL decisions, grading, misconduct, complaints and appeals.
- Safety, wellbeing and critical-incident decisions.
- Employment, legal, financial, privacy and regulatory accountability.
- Acceptance of material residual risk.

Every approval must record the decision, accountable person, evidence considered, conditions, date and review point.
