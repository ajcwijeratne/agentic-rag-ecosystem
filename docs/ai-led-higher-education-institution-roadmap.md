# WijerCo AI-led higher-education operating roadmap

Updated: 12 July 2026

## Executive finding

The workforce enhancement creates a strong AI-enabled consulting and academy operating layer. It does **not** by itself make WijerCo a higher education institution. The current repository shows no evidence of provider registration, an independent governing body and academic board, accredited courses, student administration, admissions, academic records, complaints and appeals, wellbeing and safety services, tuition protection, or an operating quality-assurance cycle.

WijerCo should make one threshold decision before investing further:

1. **AI-led higher-education consultancy and non-award academy:** deliver advisory services and professional learning without representing products as AQF qualifications. This is the nearer-term model.
2. **Registered Institute of Higher Education:** pursue TEQSA registration and course accreditation, then add HELP and CRICOS only if the business model requires them.

TEQSA's June 2026 guide says prospective providers typically need one to two years to prepare a quality initial-registration application and should approach TEQSA at least six months before submission. Registration as an Institute of Higher Education must be accompanied by accreditation of at least one course. Treat a provider pathway as a separate regulated transformation, not an agent deployment.

## Target operating model

```text
Governing body (human, independent, accountable)
├─ Academic board (human academic authority)
├─ Audit / risk / finance oversight
├─ Student voice and external expertise
└─ Chief executive / accountable officers
   └─ WijerCo Orchestrator
      ├─ Marketing & Sales
      ├─ Research & Intelligence
      ├─ Learning Design
      ├─ Academic Development
      ├─ Support
      ├─ Operations
      ├─ Academic Affairs & Registry
      ├─ Student Experience & Success
      ├─ Library & Scholarly Services
      ├─ Research & Innovation
      ├─ Governance, Risk & Assurance
      └─ People & Culture
```

The AI workforce executes analysis, drafting, monitoring and controlled workflow. It must not replace the governing body's accountability, academic judgement, procedural fairness or authorised decisions.

## 90-day build sequence

### Days 0–30: define the institution

- Approve the strategic choice between a non-award academy and a registered provider.
- Establish a human governance design, decision-rights schedule and risk appetite.
- Create the institutional obligations register and evidence repository.
- Inventory all AI use cases, data stores, integrations and external actions.
- Define success measures: educational quality, learner outcomes, service, compliance, financial sustainability and AI reliability.

### Days 31–60: operate the controls

- Run governing-body and academic-board simulations with agendas, papers, decisions and minutes.
- Pilot the orchestrator on five end-to-end workflows with human approval gates.
- Implement privacy, records, security, incident, complaints and urgent-risk workflows.
- Build the course concept and approval pack for one flagship offering, clearly labelled non-award unless accredited.
- Establish evaluation sets for routing accuracy, evidence faithfulness, bias/accessibility, privacy leakage and escalation compliance.

### Days 61–90: prove effectiveness

- Complete three evidenced operating cycles for each critical control.
- Commission independent reviews of governance design and the flagship course if pursuing registration.
- Test business continuity, AI/model outage, data breach, unsafe learner message and erroneous-regulatory-advice scenarios.
- Close findings with owners and dates; do not mark a control effective from a policy document alone.
- Produce a board decision pack for the next 12–24 months, including cost, people, systems, regulator engagement and stop/go criteria.

## AI operating controls

Every deployed agent or automation should have:

- a named human owner and approved purpose;
- authorised data sources and prohibited data;
- explicit actions it may take, actions needing approval and actions it may never take;
- test cases and acceptance thresholds before release;
- traceable sources, model/tool/version, inputs, outputs, approvals and downstream changes;
- monitoring for accuracy, drift, bias, privacy, security, accessibility, cost and service impact;
- a safe fallback, kill switch, incident path and periodic reapproval;
- retention and deletion rules consistent with the record type.

Do not use model confidence as a substitute for evidence or human authority. For consequential decisions, agents may assemble and test the evidence; the authorised person or body must make and record the decision.
